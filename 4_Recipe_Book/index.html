BreakFast

  1. Vegetable Upma

    About The Dish

    Vegetable upma is a South Indian–origin breakfast prepared using roasted semolina (rava) and vegetables. It is warm, filling, and nutritious, providing steady energy. Upma is especially good for people who want a balanced and non-oily breakfast.

    List Of Ingridents

    • Rava (suji)
    • Mixed vegetables
    • Mustard seeds
    • Curry leaves
    • Green chili

    Method of Prepration

    1. Dry roast rava and keep aside.
    2. Temper mustard seeds, curry leaves, chili → add vegetables.
    3. Add water & salt → boil → slowly add rava while stirring.
    4. Cook till soft.
    Nutrition Note: Light, quick energy, easy to digest

  2. Aloo Paratha

    About The Dish

    Aloo paratha is a North Indian stuffed flatbread made with spiced mashed potatoes. It is heavy and very satisfying, often served with curd or butter. This dish is best when you need long-lasting energy, such as during travel or physically active days.

    List Of Ingridents

    • Wheat flour
    • Boiled potato
    • Onion
    • Spices

    Method of Prepration

    1. Knead dough.
    2. Mix potato with spices.
    3. Stuff dough → roll → cook on tawa with ghee.
    Nutrition Note: Heavy & energy-dense

  4. Poha

    About The Dish

    Poha is a traditional Indian breakfast made from flattened rice. It is light on the stomach, cooks very fast, and is commonly eaten in Maharashtra and Madhya Pradesh. The addition of peanuts gives crunch and energy, while lemon improves taste and digestion. It is ideal for busy mornings.

    Ingridents

    • Flattened rice (poha)
    • Onion
    • Peanuts
    • Mustard Seeds
    • Curry leaves
    • Turmeric
    • Lemon

    Method Of Prepration

    1. Wash poha and drain.
    2. Heat oil → add mustard seeds, curry leaves, peanuts, onion.
    3. Add turmeric & salt → mix poha → cook 2–3 min.
    4. Finish with lemon & coriander.
    Nutrition Note: Light, quick energy, easy to digest

  5. Besan Chilla

    About The Dish

    Besan chilla is a savory pancake made from gram flour. It is high in protein and does not require fermentation, making it quick to prepare. Adding vegetables increases nutrition. It is a popular choice for healthy and fitness-focused diets.

    List Of Ingridents

    • Besan (gram flour),
    • Onion
    • Tomato
    • Green chili
    • Spices

    Method of Prepration

    1. Make batter with water, salt, turmeric, chili..
    2. Add chopped vegetables.
    3. Spread on hot tawa like dosa → cook both sides.
    Nutrition Note: High protein, low carb

Lunch

  1. Dal Chawal

    About The Dish

    Dal chawal is one of the most common Indian lunch meals. It is nutritious, comforting, and easy to digest. Rich in protein and carbohydrates, it provides balanced energy and is ideal for daily home meals.

    List Of Ingridents

    • Lentils (toor/moong)
    • Rice
    • Onion
    • Tomato
    • Turmeric
    • Cumin
    • Garlic

    Method of Prepration

    1. Boil dal with turmeric and salt.
    2. Prepare tadka using cumin, garlic, onion, tomato.
    3. Mix tadka into dal. Serve with steamed rice.
    Complete daily meal

  2. Rajma Chawal

    About The Dish

    Rajma chawal is a popular North Indian lunch dish. It is high in protein and fiber, making it filling and satisfying. This dish is perfect for days when you need long-lasting energy.

    List Of Ingridents

    • Rajma (kidney beans)
    • Rice
    • Onion
    • Tomato
    • Ginger-Garlic
    • Spices

    Method of Prepration

    1. Soak rajma overnight and pressure cook.
    2. Prepare onion-tomato gravy with spices.
    3. Add rajma and simmer. Serve with rice.
    Very high fiber & protein

    List Of Ingredients

    • Boiled potatoes

    • Green chili
    • Ginger
